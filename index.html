Business Equipment Finance Explained

Selecting the correct product depends on ownership goals and tax needs. A chattel mortgage allows the business to own the asset immediately, claiming the GST upfront and interest as a deduction. A finance lease keeps ownership with the lender, offering lower payments with a choice to buy or return at the end. An operating lease is ideal for technology that needs frequent upgrading, while a commercial hire purchase involves paying instalments to take ownership after the final payment. To understand these business equipment options in detail, it is essential to compare tax outcomes.

Eligible equipment and assets

Lenders provide funding for a wide variety of assets. This includes manufacturing and industrial machinery like CNC machines, construction plant such as excavators, agricultural machinery and medical or dental equipment. Commercial kitchens, refrigeration and technology hardware are also commonly financed. Many lenders also fund quality used equipment, though age limits apply and often cap eligibility around ten years old, depending on the asset type.

Tax benefits and accounting

One of the main drivers for using finance is the potential tax efficiency. With structures like a chattel mortgage, businesses can claim the GST on the purchase price in their next Business Activity Statement. The interest component of repayments is generally tax deductible, and depreciation claims can be made on the asset. The instant asset write-off scheme may also be applicable depending on the business turnover and asset value, though these rules change frequently.

Application and approval criteria

Lenders primarily assess the trading history of the business, the type of equipment being purchased and its resale value. While deposits are typically 10 to 30 per cent, established businesses with strong records may secure up to 100 per cent financing. Speed is a key advantage, with many approvals granted within 24 to 48 hours. Successful applicants must usually hold comprehensive insurance on the equipment to protect the lender's security interest.

  1. Identify requirements. Determine the specific equipment needed, whether it is new or used, and how it will generate revenue for the business.
  2. Compare structures. Review chattel mortgages, leases and hire purchases to decide which offers the best cash flow and tax outcome for your situation.
  3. Submit application. Provide trading history and asset details to a lender or broker to secure approval, often within 24 to 48 hours.

Common questions

What deposit is required for equipment finance? Deposits typically range from 10 to 30 per cent of the asset value, though established businesses with strong trading history may qualify for 100 per cent financing.

Can I finance second-hand equipment? Yes, lenders generally finance quality used equipment, although age limits apply and usually restrict eligibility to assets up to ten years old.

How long does the approval process take? Many equipment finance applications are approved within 24 to 48 hours, allowing businesses to proceed with purchases quickly.
